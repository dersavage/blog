About

I am a fellow human being, writing on philosophy, religion, mathematics, programming, and animals, especially cats and dogs, and occasionally on sports and cooking. The list may change as this site evolves.

I divide my life so far into three periods. The golden period, the dreamy period, and the period of reality. In the golden period, I was a professor of mathematics, social and natural sciences at a prestigious university in East Asia. In the dreamy period, I stopped teaching and entered the land of programming. The teacher and the programmer are still a part of me, even though I have entered the stage of reality. In this stage, I strive to have the color of colorlessness.

I hope, that if you are into any of these topics, you will find something useful here; however, I must warn you in advance that this site is primarily for my own self. I write so I can think better. I choose to make my writings public because I strive to have better thoughts, thoughts of which I should be proud of. Perhaps someday I will write a book directly addressed at the good reader, but for now, the blog entries are mostly for my own self.
